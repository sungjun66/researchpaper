LPV-MPC for Lateral Stability Enhancement of Four-wheel Steering Vehicles With Real-time Embedded PiLS Validation

Sungjun Park1, Kwangki Kim1,2,*,
1Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Inha University, Republic of Korea 2Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Inha University, Republic of Korea
Paper arXiv Video Code Data

Abstract

This paper presents a linear parameter-varying model predictive control (LPV-MPC) strategy for enhancing the lateral stability of four-wheel steering (4WS) vehicles.

Its real-time performance validated for the first time in an embedded Processor-in-the-Loop Simulation (PiLS) environment using MATLAB/Simulink and a Teensy 4.1 development board. The proposed controller is benchmarked against frontwheel steering (FWS), linear quadratic regulator (LQR), and uncontrolled cases under two representative scenarios: step steering and doublelane change maneuvers. In the step steering test, performance is evaluated in terms of maximum deviation and settling time, while in the double-lane change test, root mean square error (RMSE) is used as the primary performance metric. PiLS results confirm that all computations are completed within the sampling period, demonstrating the feasibility of real-time embedded implementation.

Compared to the uncontrolled baseline, the LPV-MPC-based 4WS controller achieves a 53.6% reduction in yaw rate RMSE and outperforms the LQR controller by 65.3% in double-lane change maneuvers while also reducing the peak-to-peak sideslip angle by 25.7%. In the step steering test, it achieves a 1.2% lower maximum deviation than FWS with a fast settling time of 3.65 s.

Control Architecture

The proposed control architecture in this paper derives the target state values to be tracked by the controller based on the steering angle commanded by the driver. The controller applies control inputs(front and rear wheel steering angles) to a virtual vehicle (plant model) in order to minimize the error between the current state and the target state. In addition, the LPV-MPC controller receives the current longitudinal velocity of the vehicle as feedback and updates the system dynamic matrices A and B of the prediction model in real time.

Verification Environment

The proposed embedded model predictive control algorithm was implemented using the OSQP solver and deployed on a Teensy 4.1 development board featuring an ARM Cortex-M7 processor operating at 600 MHz with 32-bit floating-point support, 7.75 MB of flash memory, and 512 kB of tightly coupled RAM. The virtual vehicle (plant model) was developed in Simulink based on a 7-DOF vehicle dynamics model.
